# Euterpea Studio Harmonium

A sampled harmonium instrument for [Decent Sampler](https://www.decentsamples.com/product/decent-sampler-plugin/), built from recordings of a harmonium sampled at the Euterpea Studio at Yale University's Department of Computer Science.

## Requirements

- [Decent Sampler](https://www.decentsamples.com/product/decent-sampler-plugin/) v1.12.13 or later (free)

## Installation

1. Download and install Decent Sampler.
2. Download this repository (or clone it) and place the `euterpea harmonium` folder in your Decent Sampler library folder.
3. Open Decent Sampler and load `euterpea harmonium.dspreset`.

## Features

### Keyboard Range
42 keys spanning C1–F4, matching a standard harmonium compass.

### Octave Coupler
A toggle button on the UI adds a sub-octave layer to your playing. The bottom octave is intentionally uncoupled — authentic to how real harmoniums work, since there are no notes available below the instrument's range. Drones are also uncoupled.

### Drone Stops
Three independently toggleable drone stops — C#2, D#2, and G# — sustain a continuous pitch in the background.

### Tone Knob
A low-pass filter sweeps from warm and muffled (0) to bright and open (1). Default is set to a natural, balanced tone. Also mappable via MIDI CC74.

### Reed Warmth Knob
Controls the depth of a subtle chorus effect, simulating the slight pitch beating and shimmer of multiple reeds vibrating together.

### Reverb Knob
Adds room ambience, from dry to spacious. Adjust to taste depending on your mix context.

### Bellows (Mod Wheel / CC1)
The mod wheel simulates the harmonium's bellows.

### Round Robin Samples
Three alternating samples per zone reduce the mechanical repetition of looped playback, giving a more natural feel when playing repeated notes.
